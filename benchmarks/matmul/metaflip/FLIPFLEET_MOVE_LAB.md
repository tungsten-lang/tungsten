# FlipFleet move lab

This note separates exact move identities from scheduling guesses. Every
identity below is over GF(2). A runtime candidate still has to survive the
complete matrix-multiplication tensor reconstruction before entering a fleet
bank or replacing the leader.

## Shipped: complete three/four-term span refactors

For `k=3` or `k=4` selected live terms, form independent bases of their U, V,
and W factor spans. Enumerate every nonzero factor in each span and therefore
every rank-one term in the Cartesian product. In the selected bases the local
tensor has at most `k^3` coordinates, so each candidate has an exact 27-bit or
64-bit signature. This is not a random projection.

The worker hash-matches complementary exact signatures for:

- 3→2, 3↔3, and 3→4;
- 4→3 and 4↔4.

The selected-span restriction is complete: projecting any ambient replacement
through retractions onto the three selected factor spans cannot increase its
term count. Thus a miss proves that the chosen partial tensor has no requested
replacement inside (or outside) those factor spans; it says nothing about a
different selected window or the rank of the full matrix-multiplication tensor.

Worst-case sizes are small enough to bound explicitly:

| selected terms | candidate terms | unordered pairs | exact signature |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 3 | 343 | 58,653 | 27 bits |
| 4 | 3,375 | 5,693,625 | 64 bits |

The GPU worker preserves duplicate pair signatures in an open-addressed table.
The largest table has 8,388,608 slots (about 101 MB), so span-4 runs exactly
one neighborhood per child. The coordinator rotates span-3 cardinalities as
`2,2,3,4` and span-4 as `3,3,3,4`, favoring reductions without starving
rank-neutral topology changes and the +1 escape.

### External-cancellation completion

The original splice boundary rejected a replacement term when the same term
was already live outside the selected window. That is not invalid over
GF(2): the two copies parity-cancel. Consequently a local `3→4` replacement
with one external collision is a global `−1` move, while local `3↔3` with one
collision is global `−2`. The production span-3/span-4 worker now:

- deliberately chooses factor-span windows containing an external live term;
- searches collision-containing exact presentations before generic joins;
- toggles the complete symmetric difference and scores the actual compacted
  rank; and
- reconstructs the full tensor before writing or rewarding the result.

A planted zero-circuit regression turns an exact rank-27 3×3 shoulder back
into rank 23 through a nominal `3↔3` replacement with two external
collisions. The same compact admission rule is used by absorbed low-rank
shear and SAT destroy/repair. Across the current 4×4 through 7×7 leaders, all
rank-optimal 4×4/5×5 archive presentations, and seven structurally
distant 6×6/7×7 archives, 7,334 independent
tensor/cardinality/offset jobs (span-3 wants 2/3/4 and targeted span-4 wants
3/4) completed with zero failures and zero real collision hits. This is newly
enabled coverage for future basins, not a claimed rank drop on the present
frontiers.

## Next exact operators

### 1. Global one-axis kernel shear

Assign every participating term one mutable axis and solve

```text
Σ δu_i ⊗ v_i ⊗ w_i
⊕ Σ u_i ⊗ δv_i ⊗ w_i
⊕ Σ u_i ⊗ v_i ⊗ δw_i = 0.
```

Replacing the chosen factors by their XOR with the deltas is already a finite
exact move: no term changes two factors, so there are no quadratic or cubic
remainders. Applying the deltas twice reverses it. The three-term relation
above is the smallest useful example. A production lane should quotient the
kernel by term gauges, global matrix-multiplication isotropy, and the span of
ordinary pair flips before spending continuations on it.

This is now implemented as a bounded whole-frontier pure-Tungsten
elimination, rather than the earlier at-most-24-term window prototype. The
caller supplies an explicit scratch-word cap; every dependency is
materialized, one-flip endpoints are rejected, and a pool child rebuilds,
serializes, reparses, and fully verifies the complete scheme before admission.
This first bounded version does not claim to quotient the entire component
generated by sequences of ordinary flips or the full matrix-multiplication
isotropy group. Consequently the eight endpoints below are new to one flip,
not eight proven new identity classes; archive distance and downstream reward
decide whether they are useful basins.

On the checked-in 5×5 rank-93/d1155 frontier, 64 deterministic fixed,
striped, sparse, and mixed axis plans produced eight beyond-one-flip endpoints
changing three or four live terms. All eight passed the independent full
`n^6` gate. Repeated sweeps took about 0.7–0.8 seconds, but produced no rank drop and no density
improvement. Seven plans on 4×4, seven on 6×6, and four on 7×7 produced no
novel endpoint; the 7×7 sweep also demonstrated why this should not be a
general lane (the four-plan process peaked near 819 MB because scratch lived
until process exit). Therefore the
adaptive pool enables one 32-lane-equivalent CPU child only on 5×5. It is a
measured basin-diversity operator, not a record claim.

### 2. Low-rank shear with correction absorption

Choose `q` live terms `(a_i,b_i,c_i)` and a nonzero U-axis shift `p`. Let

```text
M = Σ b_i ⊗ c_i = Σ(j=1..r) y_j ⊗ z_j
```

be an exact GF(2) rank factorization. Then

```text
{(a_i,b_i,c_i)}
    ↔ {(a_i⊕p,b_i,c_i)} ∪ {(p,y_j,z_j)}
```

is an exact `q→q+r` move, because both sides differ by `p⊗M`. This generalizes
a split (`q=r=1`) to a far, low-debt escape. More importantly, if a live
carrier `(p,y_j,d_j)` exists, absorb the correction by replacing it with
`(p,y_j,d_j⊕z_j)`. With all corrections absorbed, the move is rank-neutral
and can change `2(q+r)` terms atomically. The real distance-six relation is
the `q=2,r=1` case.

Search low-rank complementary sums (`r=0,1,2`) on the GPU, but construct the
rank factorization and perform exact admission on the host. This is a better
large-support continuation of span-3 than blindly enlarging the complete
Cartesian span to five or six selected terms.

This operator now has both a host reference and a production GPU pool worker.
For q=2, Metal regularly enumerates source pair, ordered axis pair, and first
carrier; rank-two threads scan for the second carrier. The host retains
deterministic tuple order, rejects ordinary one-flip endpoints, materializes
the correction, and performs local plus full `n^6` checks. On the checked-in
5×5 rank-93/d1155 scheme, a 512-pair dispatch examines 285,696 tuples and
finds an exact non-one-flip absorbed shear at pair 504. The endpoint is
rank-neutral, so this is positive basin-diversity evidence rather than a new
rank or density result. The adaptive pool enables it on 5×5–7×7 and keeps
4×4 off.

### 3. Linear images of primitive circuit templates

For a small tensor-zero circuit `Σ a_i⊗b_i⊗c_i=0` and arbitrary linear maps
`P,Q,R`, its image remains zero. Precompute primitive 5–12-term templates
modulo the three general-linear groups and axis permutations, fit two or three
template terms to live terms, then score the full image for overlap, density,
and novelty on the GPU. Zero images vanish and duplicate images cancel.

This is broader than the current nearby-factor circuit miner and broader than
the old coordinate-only Strassen motif. It is the most plausible template
lane for flip-isolated 4×4.

The first complete two-anchor closure is now implemented in
`flipfleet_circuit_image_search.w`.  It fits every ordered live-term pair to
every anchor pair in the five 2-dimensional primitive templates, requires the
induced maps to be injective on the factor spans actually used, and then
scores the guaranteed exact circuit image with constant-time live-term hash
lookups.  The closure covered 16 independent 4×4/5×5/6×6/7×7 frontiers,
47,216,180 fits, in 7.3 loaded-host seconds.  There was no neutral or
rank-lowering image.  Best exact shoulders were 4×4 rank 49 (distance 6,
density 455), 5×5 rank 94 (distance 5, density 979), 6×6 rank 154 (distance 5,
density 2515 on the d2502 basin), and 7×7 rank 248 (distance 5, density 3107).

An equal-move 5×5 continuation compared the template-fitted +1 shoulder with
an ordinary random split: eight trials per arm and five million moves per
trial returned to rank 93 in 8/8 versus 8/8, found no rank-92 or density win,
and had identical average/max source distances 10/18.  Template returns were
changed basins in 8/8 versus 7/8 controls, too small a distinction to justify
another live lane.  Keep the fast exact fitter as an offline shoulder bank and
planted regression; revisit only with three-anchor overlap evidence or a
downstream reward advantage.

### 4. Flattening-gauge repartition

For a `k`-term window, flatten it as `P=U K^T`, where the columns of `K` are
`v_i⊗w_i`. For any `G∈GL(k,2)`, set `U'=UG` and `K'=KG^{-T}`. Factor every
reshaped column of `K'` exactly as a GF(2) matrix; the sum of those matrix ranks
is the new CP rank. Beam-search sparse transvections in `G`, alternating all
three flattenings, and retain ranks `k`, `k+1`, and `k+2`.

This reaches 8–16-term windows without enumerating `(2^k-1)^3` candidates.
Ordinary flips are the special case in which every transformed matrix remains
rank one.

The pure-Tungsten beam, compact external-collision admission, planted
multi-term/exact-full-scheme tests, and deterministic scanner are now
implemented. A bounded depth-four/beam-32 audit made 192 all-axis probes and
expanded 1,396,800 gauge states: 4×4/d450 used sixteen random windows each at
`k=8` and `k=12`, while 5×5/d968 and 6×6/d1860 used sixteen `k=8` windows.
Every probe found an exact changed presentation, but 190 were rank-plus-one
shoulders. The two rank-neutral 5×5 endpoints were both denser (best delta
+2); there were no rank drops, density wins, or external collisions. This is
useful executable coverage for larger structured windows, but current
frontiers do not justify a hot lane. Keep it as a low-cadence/offline scout,
especially for newly discovered 7×7 leaf presentations rather than repeatedly
scanning the same normalized leaders.

### 5. Universal telescoping bridge and path braids

Any two terms `(a,c,e)` and `(b,v,s)` obey

```text
(a,c,e) ⊕ (b,v,s)
⊕ (a,c,e⊕s) ⊕ (a,c⊕v,s) ⊕ (a⊕b,v,s) = 0.
```

This gives six ordered 2→3 paths. Exchanging two paths gives an exact
rank-neutral 3↔3 braid. Algebraically this is donor-factor structured-plus
followed by a forced flip, so it is not a new identity class; its possible
value is atomic completion. Exhaustively scoring all six paths costs only
`6*C(R,2)`. Keep it as a small control rather than a major allocation because
the older broad structured-plus family was neutral.

### 6. Overlapping block-parity swaps

Restricted multiplication tensors `B(I,J,K)` are trilinear in the three index
set indicators. Any small GF(2) zero identity among the indicator triples lifts
to a zero identity among overlapping rectangular multiplication tensors.
Substituting the best exact rectangular decomposition for each block yields a
large exact macro move between leaf layouts.

Current block composition only partitions into disjoint leaves. Overlap and
parity cancellation are therefore genuinely new. `flipfleet_overlapping_block_frontier.w`
now searches this family without allocating a full endpoint first: live term
supports, complements, and bounded AND/OR/XOR closure form the mask banks; the
best exact rectangular leaf from the checked-in 2--8 pool is embedded in each
block; missing minimum-dimension-two shapes are completed by fully gated
disjoint tensor splits (for example an exact rank-81 2×7×7 leaf); a hash-parity
pass computes the exact resulting term-set rank; and only
rank-neutral/lower estimates reach the complete coefficient gate. A planted
rank-47 shoulder proves that the bounded scanner rediscovers and removes a
known zero macro.

The complete coordinate-singleton catalogue is a useful negative boundary.
It has 432, 4,050, and 9,261 four-cycles at sizes 4, 6, and 7 respectively,
and every one compacts to the empty macro: the fixed width-one axis forces all
four restrictions to remain schoolbook, so no 2--8 leaf can change either
side. For wider masks, one canonical representative of every cardinality
makes the full-tensor-block branch complete modulo coordinate permutations.
A 32-mask support bank, 512 evenly spaced identities per axis, and the best 32
single macros gave:

| frontier | best fully gated full-block replacement | best single estimate | best pair-XOR estimate |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 4×4 rank 47 | 54 | 54 | 61 |
| 6×6 rank 153 | 165 | 160 | 164 |
| 7×7 rank 247/d3098 | 257 | 262 | 267 |
| 7×7 rank 247/d3554 | 257 | 262 | 267 |

The 7×7 support-guided macros shared no term with either live presentation;
the best 6×6 macro shared four terms but still incurred rank debt seven. No
rank-neutral endpoint survived, so this move is documented and tested but is
not placed in the live CPU/GPU pool. The reproducible audit is
`flipfleet_overlapping_block_frontier_bench.w`; the broad run takes roughly
nine seconds on the development machine and does not touch the TUI or a running
fleet.

### 7. Affine Segre cubes

If all three factors depend affinely on `x∈GF(2)^4`, then the XOR of the 16
rank-one terms over the affine cube is zero: every coordinate is a Boolean
polynomial of degree at most three, whose sum over a four-cube vanishes. This
is an exact 16-term escape family. Current records have too few fixed cubes for
the homogeneous 15-term specialization to be attractive, so keep it confined
to fixed-cube-rich symmetry shoulders.

## Speculative, isolated experiments

- **Quotiented tangent closure:** after removing gauges, isotropy, ordinary
  flip tangents, and one-axis shears, solve the remaining finite quadratic and
  cubic residual by SAT or a GPU syndrome walk. The raw Jacobian excess is not
  itself evidence of a new move.
- **Labeled canceling-pair catalyst:** inject two labeled copies of the same
  term at rank `R+2`, braid them through exact flips, then require annihilation.
  The labels change the search state, not the underlying algebra.
- **Residual worm:** maintain `S⊕G=T` with a small exact defect carrier `G`,
  mutate `S` aggressively, compress `G`, and seek `G=0`.
- **GF(4) detour:** search Frobenius-closed conjugate pairs and return to
  individually GF(2) factors. Conversion cost makes this last priority.
- **Projection/completion holonomy:** walk a rectangular projection and return
  through a different exact boundary completion. Do this only after an exact
  overlapping-block completion engine exists.

## Rank-one matroid-circuit exchange audit (2026-07-14)

`flipfleet_matroid_circuit.w` treats every nonzero one-bit factor
perturbation as a matroid column. For example, replacing `u` by `u+e_p` has
the exact tensor delta `e_p (x) v (x) w`. A 63-bit linear tensor sketch drives
a bounded 2+1 or 2+2 meet-in-the-middle join, but it is only a filter: every
hit is checked over all factor-space coordinates, applied atomically on
distinct live terms, parity-compacted, and locally reverified. The real
benchmark then rebuilds and full-gates the complete matrix-multiplication
tensor. A planted four-column test also verifies a density-decreasing
`-4` circuit, so the negative frontier result is not a dead implementation.

The exhaustive three-column audit gave the following results:

| frontier | edit columns | distinct-term pairs | exact circuits | result |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 4x4 rank 47 / d450 | 2,227 | 2,426,994 | 0 | no endpoint |
| 5x5 rank 93 / d1155 | 6,942 | 23,836,572 | 3 | all complete span-3; best d1158 |
| 5x5 rank 93 / d968 | 6,947 | 23,870,926 | 1 | complete span-3; d969 |

## Five-changed-term 2+3 syndrome join (2026-07-14)

`flipfleet_matroid_circuit5.w` extends the column neighborhood from singleton
bit edits to arbitrary one-factor XOR masks. For every live term and axis it
includes all one-bit toggles, every collapse of a factor of weight at least
three to one of its live singleton bits, XOR by each of the three closest live
factors, and direct transplant to each such factor. A deterministic reservoir
can be negative-only, 2/3 negative, balanced, 1/3 negative, or nonnegative-
only. The exact engine stores the pair syndromes and probes triples, using the
unique partition whose stored pair is the two smallest selected positions.

This is a genuinely new direct operator class, not a renamed production
move. The complete span lane ends at 3<->3/4 and 4<->4, while the MITM and
k-XOR lanes are rank-lowering 5->4 through 9->8 searches. The new endpoint is
rank-neutral 5->5 and can have term-set distance five. The global one-axis
kernel-shear equation contains it abstractly, but the production child emits
the first dependency of a bounded axis plan rather than performing this
sparse minimal-circuit join.

The focused test plants the minimal rank-one circuit

```text
(1,1) + (1,2) + (1,4) + (2,7) + (3,7) = 0
```

on five distinct terms. The join recovers it, changes all five terms, lowers
density by five, and is outside even the old five-factor spans. A second test
adds both sides of a shifted copy to the exact 3x3 rank-23 scheme, obtaining
an exact rank-33 shoulder; applying the five-term move parity-cancels the ten
planted terms and returns to rank 23. Fresh worker reconstruction and the
complete `n^6` gate both pass. Proper zero subcircuits of size two or three
are rejected, so a span-3 circuit plus an unrelated pair cannot masquerade as
a new size-five hit.

The real-frontier audit was negative:

|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 4x4 rank 47 / d450 | 3,064 | over 413 million | 0 |
| 4x4 rank 47 / d677 | 3,464 | over 102 million | 0 |
| 5x5 rank 93 / d968 | 8,695 | over 230 million | 0 |
| 5x5 rank 93 / d1155 | 9,011 | over 208 million | 0 |
| 7x7 rank 247 / d3098 | 41,819 | over 213 million | 0 |

The d450 evidence includes an exhaustive negative-only run: all 1,102
density-decreasing moves, 592,919 admissible pair syndromes, and 206,398,992
distinct-term triples. Thus this bounded neighborhood contains no
five-negative circuit on that frontier, not merely no hit in a random sample.
Across all policies and frontiers the campaign exceeded 1.16 billion triple
probes without one 63-bit syndrome collision. The sketch is only a filter,

five-term windows selected by a low-rank flattening dependency--rather than
merely increasing this reservoir.

## Whole-scheme `GL` isotropy: normalize, do not randomize (2026-07-14)

A global isotropy word is a rank-neutral exact reparametrization, not a new
tensor identity. Random words therefore create different raw term sets and
different D3/reversal hashes while remaining in the same known `GL` orbit.
The 4×4 rank-47/d450 audit changed all 24 raw IDs but produced no rank or
density win after 24 × 2M moves. Random 5×5 images were usually denser and
lost to untransformed controls. Do not reward random global images as archive
novelty.

The useful move is deterministic density descent over elementary global swaps

separate measured orbit classes, but equal signatures do not certify

## Projection/replacement tunnels: exact, but covered (2026-07-14)

arbitrary-cardinality subset that can be transformed atomically. Production
requires weight at least five, parity compacts the endpoint, reconstructs the
entire matrix-multiplication tensor, and then quotients it by exact term-set
comparison with both the source and the whole-scheme `phi` image.

## Raw one-factor map nullspaces (2026-07-14)

pool slot should be assigned to raw one-factor maps on the current leaders.

## Large-bank affine-code decoding (2026-07-14)

`flipfleet_affine_code_descent.w` treats canonical rank-one terms as binary
coordinates.  Given exact schemes `x0, xi` for the same tensor, every
`di = x0 XOR xi` is a zero tensor, so every word in the affine code
`x0 + span(di)` is exact without a nonlinear repair.  The implementation
interns the term union once, stores contiguous `i64` generator rows, removes

start with an ordinary compatible-pair flip.

## Cubic three-factor map nullspace (2026-07-14)

`flipfleet_three_factor_map_nullspace.w` closes the raw-map hierarchy left by
the one- and two-factor audits. For fixed raw maps `A,B,C`, each term receives

```text

```
