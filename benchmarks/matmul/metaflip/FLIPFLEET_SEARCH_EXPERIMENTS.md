# FlipFleet search experiments (2026-07-13)

This note records the nine search changes added after the initial adaptive
CPU/GPU portfolio.  The live campaign path is pure Tungsten: the coordinator,
CPU experiments, Metal hosts, cache builder, scheduler, exact gates, and TUI
all run from `flipfleet_native.w` and its Tungsten dependencies.  Checked-in
Metal is either emitted from those Tungsten workers or compiled ahead of an
epoch; Python tools in this directory are offline research aids only.

Every candidate that could enter a bank, archive, MAP-Elites cell, or the
rank/density frontier is still exhaustively reconstructed.  Canonical hashes,
128-bit projections, and planted tests are filters or diagnostics, never
tensor-rank proofs.

## 1. Cached metallibs and persistent GPU workers

`flipfleet_metallib_cache.w` builds and atomically publishes a `.metallib` next
to each native worker.  Freshness is tied to the emitted Metal source.  The
cache covers square and rectangular cal2zone, C3 and standalone C3×Z2, SIMD,
MITM, k-XOR/circuit, and constraint-pool workers; source compilation remains a
developer fallback.

The default one-round GPU epochs also keep stable square-generic and
rectangular cal2zone children alive through generation-numbered command and
acknowledgement mailboxes.  A child owns its Metal device, library, pipeline,
queue, and buffers across commands, but rereads and exact-gates the requested
seed and output each time.  A lane-count change restarts that child.  C3,
SIMD, and rotating-pool jobs remain bounded children but load cached
metallibs, preserving their existing barriers, retry policy, and accounting.

Startup-dominated 3×3 measurements put a source-compiling child at about
122 ms per tiny launch, a cached child at 64 ms, and a persistent command at
15–19 ms across focused samples (the latest integration sample was 19 ms).
These numbers measure launch/rotation latency, not a change in kernel move
throughput.  See [`GPU_METALLIB_CACHE.md`](GPU_METALLIB_CACHE.md).

## 2. Live primitive-circuit mining

Pool mode 11 (`primitive-5plus`) performs regular Metal pair/triple and
triple/triple joins over a live factor closure.  A projected zero is accepted
only after the host verifies the complete zero tensor, verifies every proper
nonempty subset is nonzero, toggles the identity into the live scheme, and
reconstructs the complete matrix-multiplication tensor.

A real 3×3 smoke starting from the exact rank-23/density-139 frontier emitted
an exact rank-29 alternate escape.  That demonstrates live circuit discovery
and a genuinely different seed; it is not a rank improvement or lower bound.

## 3. Cross-parent differential surgery

Pool mode 12 (`parent-diff`) chooses a far companion for the primary seed from
MAP-Elites/archive state and works in the parents' symmetric difference.  Its
primary path expands every difference term into a complete bit-packed n^6
column, performs exact GF(2) elimination, and scores bounded combinations of
proper nullspace relations as hybrid splices.  The older pair/triple primitive
five-circuit join remains as a fallback when the complete difference exceeds
the bound.  Both parents and every child are exhaustively gated.  The scheduler
permits at most one such CPU child, represented by one 32-lane logical quantum;
unused GPU allocation returns to the ordinary roles.

Beyond the planted rank-32/rank-32 → rank-27 control, the real 5×5 rank-93
d1155/d1168 pair has a 16-term difference of exact nullity three.  The worker
selected six exclusive terms from each parent and emitted a third exact
rank-93/density-1165 decomposition.  This is useful live basin diversity, but
not a rank or density improvement.

## 4. Staged 8→7 and 9→8 surgery

Pool modes 13 and 14 extend k-XOR surgery with Metal triple/quad and quad/quad
enumeration.  Candidate factor families are deliberately capped at 16 and
each mode at 128 lanes.  The 128-bit join is only a prefilter; complete local
and spliced-scheme reconstruction remains authoritative.

Planted 6→5, 7→6, 8→7, and 9→8 controls all recovered an exact rank-27 parent
from an exact rank-28 split seed.  The 8→7/9→8 results validate the staged
engines, but do not establish local minimality when a bounded search misses
and do not improve a world record by themselves.

## 5. The bounded 6×6 C3×Z2 experiment

The standalone worker preserves six images generated by C3 rotation and
simultaneous index reversal.  The implementation and older notes call it D3
for brevity; the actual commuting generators here form C3×Z2.

Four trials per arm used 128 walkers × 5,000 steps, for 2.56 million
configured transitions per arm.  Both constrained engines returned rank 153
in all four trials and neither found rank 152:

| arm | final densities | elapsed / attempted rate |
|---|---|---:|
| C3 | `2502, 2502, 2502, 2511` | 4.671 s / 548k steps/s |
| C3×Z2 | `2502, 2502, 2502, 2502` | 7.963 s / 321k steps/s |
| reversal break + generic | `2502, 2502, 2539, 2528` | same 2.56M configured steps |

The six-image engine delivered about 59% of C3 throughput and did not improve
the density-2502 leader.  It therefore has no default continuous lane and is
not in the rotating production pool.  The exact worker remains available as
a standalone opt-in experiment; deliberate symmetry breaking is the faster
default route.  The ratio is directional because an unrelated 7×7 campaign
shared the GPU during measurement.  Full gates and parameters are in
[`FLIPFLEET_D3_6X6.md`](FLIPFLEET_D3_6X6.md).

## 6. Symmetry-canonical basin identities

`flipfleet_basin_identity.w` computes a dual 31-bit, order-independent digest
over the twelve images formed by the six valid tensor-axis automorphisms and
simultaneous index reversal.  It also incorporates sorted per-axis histograms
of GF(2) matrix ranks, a cheap invariant under invertible basis changes.

The identity prevents axis/cyclic/reversal copies from being counted as new
basins in bank distance, MAP-Elites niches, active-island diversity, and
lineage.  It is a 62-bit telemetry key, not an exact equivalence certificate:
hash equality never replaces exhaustive tensor reconstruction.  Regression
tests apply axis, C3, reflection, and reversal transforms to an exact 3×3
scheme and require the same identity and GL histogram, while a split shoulder
must differ.

## 7. Delayed lineage rewards

`flipfleet_lineage.w` tags direct GPU roles, pool modes, and rectangular
components, then carries that provenance through MAP-Elites and shoulder-bank
copies using the canonical basin identity.  A bounded 256-entry registry
survives strict rank drops and is cleared by an explicit naive-frontier reset.

When a CPU island produces an exact descendant, it can credit its GPU/kernel
ancestor once: 10,000 units per rank dropped, a proportional same-rank density
reward, and 250 units for a new canonical basin.  Credit feeds the role,
epoch, contextual transition, and pool-mode reward paths.  Return to the
origin basin is tracked separately as a hazard.  This makes scheduling learn
from a GPU escape that only becomes useful after a CPU continuation, without
mistaking repeated descendants for independent rewards.

## 8. One-island CPU parameter racing

With the normal `islands` strategy and at least four CPU workers, exactly one
non-leader, non-core island races eight bounded arms:

`baseline`, `quick-narrow`, `slow-deep`, `flip-only`, `dense-work`,
`fast-band`, `short-splits`, and `marathon`.

The arms vary work/wander quotas, split cadence, reduction-pressure ceiling
and period, band growth, the work/wander threshold, and maximum band.  Every
arm receives cold coverage before an integer utility favors rank drops and
new canonical basins while penalizing return to origin.  Arm changes occur at
the existing basin lease/cycle boundary, so the other islands retain their
stable sticky-door hot loops.  This is an online experiment framework; no arm
has yet earned a general “best” claim.

## 9. One-island accepted-state cycle detector

A second bounded island maintains a rolling Zobrist digest only when a move is
accepted and probes a fixed 512-entry direct-mapped recent-state filter.  It
reports unique observations, repeats, immediate inverses, and estimated
overhead in the existing source field.  Other workers pay only the existing
mode-bit branch and do not maintain the hash.  The detector is observational;
it does not reject or redirect a move.

On the 5×5 density-1155 seed, one 20M-move native comparison measured
36,900,369 moves/s for the baseline and 37,174,721 moves/s for the watched
lane, reported as 0% overhead at integer precision.  A final-audit rerun beside
the live 7×7 campaign measured 29,940,119 versus 29,542,097 moves/s, or 1%.
Both watched runs observed 871,604 unique accepted-state fingerprints, 69,331
recent-filter hits, and 55,574 immediate inverses.  The honest result is
therefore 0–1% observed overhead in these samples; the apparent speedup in the
first is benchmark noise, not a throughput claim.

## 10. Complete local factor-span refactoring

Pool modes 15 and 16 select three or four live terms, construct exact bases of
their three factor spans, and enumerate the complete nonzero Cartesian product.
The resulting local tensor has at most 27 or 64 coordinates, so the Metal join
uses an exact `i64` signature rather than the projected 128-bit filters used by
the larger k-XOR modes.

Mode 15 rotates 3→2, 3↔3, and 3→4. Mode 16 rotates 4→3 and 4↔4. The latter is
forced to one neighborhood: a full four-dimensional window has 3,375 candidate
terms, 5,693,625 unordered pairs, and an 8,388,608-slot duplicate-preserving
table (about 101 MB). Both workers reject unchanged equal-rank permutations,
materialize the exact replacement on the host, and perform the full `n^6`
coefficient gate before writing output.

All five directions have planted Metal regressions. A rank-24 split shoulder
is reduced back to the exact rank-23 3×3 scheme through the complete worker.
More importantly for topology, the 5×5 density-1155 record contains a real
rank-neutral distance-six three-term shear that no single compatible pair flip
can express. The endpoint remains rank 93 but has density 1158, so it is a
validated basin-diversity edge rather than an objective improvement. A cached
metallib test takes about 0.09 s on the development M5 Max; source compilation
plus the same planted suite takes about 0.29 s. The integrated native fleet
built the new worker/metallib and completed a 3×3 GPU smoke with
`gpu_degraded=0`.

## 11. GPU low-rank correction absorption

Pool mode 17 implements the q=2 rank-neutral shear from the move lab. One
Metal thread owns a rotated source pair, one of six ordered axis assignments,
and a rotated first carrier. It derives the exact rank-1/rank-2 complementary
matrix factorization; rank-two threads scan for their second carrier. Every
structural match is left in shared output memory. The host scans matches in
deterministic tuple order, rejects ordinary one-flip endpoints, materializes
the correction, verifies the local identity, and finally performs the full
`n^6` splice gate.

The real 5×5 rank-93/d1155 frontier produces the first useful endpoint at
source pair 504. A 512-pair launch covers 285,696 tuples, reports two
structural hits, and admits the first non-one-flip exact rank-neutral shear.
The emitted-Metal and cached-metallib regressions both pass, as does the full
optimized fleet build. The mode is enabled for 5×5–7×7, with 256 logical
lanes at 5×5 and a provisional 128-lane cap at 6×6/7×7; 4×4 stays disabled.

## 12. Directed global-isotropy normalization

`flipfleet_global_isotropy.w` applies invertible whole-scheme swaps and
transvections with inverse replay and exhaustive exact gates. Random basis
images are deliberately not scheduled: 4×4 controls showed that they change
raw basin hashes while staying in a known `GL` orbit, and 5×5 controls found
that random words usually worsen density. Instead, a bounded steepest descent
selects only generators that reduce total factor bits, then hands the exact
endpoint back to the ordinary worker.

This combination materially changed the fixed-rank frontiers: 5×5 rank 93 now
has density 968, 6×6 rank 153 density 1860, and 7×7 rank 247 density 3098.
The independent rectangular implementation reduced ⟨2,3,4⟩ rank 20 from
density 136 to 130. Production uses it at startup to add bounded exact archive
images and immediately before adopting a promising exact CPU/GPU result. A
coarse `gi` invariant is appended to provenance so basis relabelings are not
silently presented as independent basin evidence; it is telemetry, never an
exact orbit classifier or an admission shortcut.
