# Third-party research and seed data

Tungsten Metaflip's source license does not automatically relicense imported
matrix-decomposition data. Every seed selected for a public package must carry
an asset-level record of its original file, author or discoverer, year,
upstream revision, content digest, and redistribution terms.

## Research attribution

The engine builds on the flip-graph line of work, especially:

- Manuel Kauers and Jakob Moosbauer, *Flip Graphs for Matrix Multiplication*.
- Manuel Kauers and J. E. H. Wood, *The Meta Flip Graph*.
- Jakob Moosbauer and Julian Poole, *The Symmetric Flip Graph*.

These citations acknowledge algorithms and scientific context; they do not
imply that paper text or source code was copied into this bit.

## Known upstream data sources

The research checkout currently draws some starting decompositions from these
upstreams:

- [`jakobmoosbauer/flips`](https://github.com/jakobmoosbauer/flips), whose
  repository states GPL-3.0-or-later. In particular, the local
  `matmul_4x4x5_rank60_gf2.txt` lineage begins with a normalized conversion of
  `solutions/445-60-mod2.exp`. It is not covered by this bit's MIT/Apache
  notice. Do not place that imported file in a dual-licensed release archive
  without an explicit redistribution decision and the required GPL material.
- [`dronperminov/FastMatrixMultiplication`](https://github.com/dronperminov/FastMatrixMultiplication),
  an MIT-licensed catalog used for some normalized rectangular seeds, with its
  upstream discoverer attribution retained.
- [`solven-eu/matmulcatalog`](https://github.com/solven-eu/matmulcatalog), a
  catalog used for pinned GF(2) and field-sensitive comparison schemes. Verify
  the license of the exact pinned revision and record per-file provenance
  before bundling any of its data.

Some catalog entries attribute the underlying decomposition to AlphaTensor,
AlphaEvolve, Hopcroft--Kerr, Kauers--Moosbauer, or other discoverers. Preserve
that scientific attribution even when a repository license permits
redistribution.

### FastMatrixMultiplication 2x2x9 seed

`matmul_2x2x9_rank32_d156_perminov_2025_gf2.txt` is a normalized GF(2)
reduction of Andrew I. Perminov's
`schemes/known/tensor/2x2x9_tensor.mpl`. The source first appears in upstream
commit `5d6eebda0257395caff3d2f8a93837d4e3a499bf` (2025-11-26); it was imported
from catalog revision `e0ec7db4cb7d7ca41abbb2c6e3bd8c7de75c7c64`. The upstream source SHA-256 is
`9b4e882a17f95962bd53792e628afe166c4922a109d58b73c50f7d38f3e58b91`.
Normalization reduces signed coefficients modulo two and transposes the
output-dual factor from upstream `(k,i)` order to Metaflip `(i,k)` bit order.
The resulting rank-32/density-156 certificate SHA-256 is
`4fa184fa84e381fdc261ed1a7d61559d5167252c7b7dac8ecb4c5fb5ddcd3989`.
Both implementations independently reconstruct all 1,296 coefficients. The
two adjacent `pperm` seeds are simultaneous coordinate permutations of this
certificate for restart diversity, not separately discovered algorithms.
FastMatrixMultiplication is distributed under the MIT License, copyright
Andrew Perminov (2025).

## Release rule

Keep an imported scheme outside the bit when its redistribution status is not
settled. The fleet may load such a scheme from the user's live store, while the
curated `tungsten-metaflip-results` repository records the certificate and its
provenance under terms appropriate to that asset. Derived fleet output should
retain its parent lineage in the public metadata even when the numerical
decomposition is independently verified.
